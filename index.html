VIEWING THE EU SOCIOECONOMIC TERRAIN FOR AMERICAN GRADUATE EDUCATION

Objectives

  • equip graduate students in cybersecurity, international relations, and related fields with an in-depth understanding of the EU’s approach to economic security, hybrid threats, and resilience in the context of global challenges;
  • highlight the role of gender equality as a critical pillar of EU socioeconomic policies, fostering discussions on empowering women in high-tech, business, and educational sectors within the EU and Ukraine;
  • explore the role of language, cultural sensitivity, and national identity in fostering socioeconomic engagement and integration within the EU, with a particular focus on Eastern Europe and Ukraine;
  • deliver interactive, interdisciplinary training sessions that combine perspectives from cybersecurity, economics, and cultural studies to provide students with actionable knowledge and critical thinking skills.

Description

The "Viewing the EU Socioeconomic Terrain for American Graduate Education (VANTAGE)" project spans 36 months and is structured around interdependent stages that ensure systematic progress and measurable outcomes. The PERT chart illustrates the flow and timing of the project's key activities.

Project Management and Coordination (Month 1–36). This stage provides the foundation for the project's success. It encompasses activities such as team coordination, budget and timeline management, and reporting to the EU. Effective coordination ensures that all other stages proceed smoothly and remain aligned with the project's objectives.

Content Development (Month 1–12). During the first year, teaching materials, including thematic modules, a digital textbook, and supplementary resources, are developed. These materials are designed to address topics like EU economic security, hybrid threats, and gender equality. This stage ensures the project’s academic resources are ready for delivery in subsequent stages.

Teaching and Training Delivery (Year 1, 2, 3). Teaching sessions will occur annually, focusing on graduate students specializing in cybersecurity, international relations, and related fields. These interactive and interdisciplinary sessions provide participants with theoretical and practical insights into EU policies. This stage is a cornerstone of the project, with its repeated implementation ensuring a wide-reaching impact.

Annual Workshop (Year 1, 2, 3). A workshop will be organized each year to engage policymakers, academics, and other stakeholders. These workshops focus on deepening discussions around EU economic security and related challenges, fostering collaboration and exchange of best practices.

Dissemination and Outreach (Month 1–36). Dissemination activities run throughout the project’s duration to maximize its visibility and impact. This includes maintaining a project website, publishing articles, organizing public lectures, and using social media platforms to reach a broader audience. These activities ensure that the project outcomes benefit both academic and non-academic stakeholders.

Monitoring and Evaluation (Month 1–36). Monitoring and evaluation are continuous processes, ensuring that each activity aligns with the project's objectives. Quantitative and qualitative indicators, such as participant feedback and knowledge assessments, will be used to measure success. This stage guarantees that the project achieves its short- and long-term goals while offering actionable recommendations for sustainability.
